Systematic procedure followed in Scientific report


• Information given report has to be concise but sufficient to be intelligeble and to prove conclusion satisfactory
• Information given in report should have wordings of interference that are standardised
• Standardised increases the clarity and nullifies or avoids the chances of misinterception
• Conclusion should be written prominently that it has to either written bold or should be type capital letters
• Reports should be illustrated with experimental data, photograph whenever necessary
• Conclusion forms most important part of report
• Can be definite, indefinite or no conclusion at all
• There is nothing wrong giving opinion without conclusion that is due to lack of adequate (insufficient), degeneration or decomposition of samples
