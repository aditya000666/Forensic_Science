GEQD History
• British government of Bengal felt the necessity of identifying handwiritng on secret documents connected with Indian Independence movement and created post of Government Handwriting Expert of Bengal
• First Document Examiner Expert: Mr. C.R. Hardless in 1904
• Setup shifted to Shimla in 1906 under control of Director, CID
• Later replaced by Mr. Brewster, police officer from West Bengal, CID designated as Government Examiner of Questioned Documents
• World's second older forensic institution after Scotland Yard
• Second GEQD established in Kolkata 1964 supposed to serve Eastern and Central states of country
• Third GEQD established in Hyderabad 1969 supposed to cater Southern and Western states
• GEQD Shimla alongwith Chandigarh unit merged with CFSL Chandigarh under control of Director of CFSL, Chandigarh
