Responsibilities


• Investigative jurisdiction of FBI extends to most federal criminal laws in more than 200 areas including computer crime, organised crime, piracy and hijacking, sabotage, sedition, terrorism and treason
• Bureau is principal federal agency responsible for counterintelligence, represented on United States Intelligence board body created by National Security Council
• Areas relating to domestic security, FBI is responsbile for correlating intelligence and disseminating it to other federal agencies
• Investigates violation of federal civil rights law such as racial discrimination in employment and voting and police brutality
• Bureau annually publishes a comprehensive summary of criminal activity in United States
• Collect evidence in most civil cases in which United States is or may be party and investigates individual who are being considered for employment in sensitive positions within federal government
• Enforces federal law and investigate variety of criminal activity including terrorism, cyber crime, white collar crime, public corruption, civil rights violation and other major crimes
• FBI Criminal Justice division is high tech hub providing range of state of art tools and services to law enforcement, national security and intelligence community partners and general public
• FBI provide variety of services, information and training involving biometrics
• In effort to harness new technologies and improve identification the Bureau developed its Next Generation Identification (NGI) system which provides criminal justice community with world largest and efficient electronic repository of biometric and criminal history information
