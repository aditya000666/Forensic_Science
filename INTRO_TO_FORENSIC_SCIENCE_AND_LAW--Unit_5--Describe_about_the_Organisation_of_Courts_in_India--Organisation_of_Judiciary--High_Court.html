High Court


• Sitation and highest court at every State and Union Territories
• Presently 24 courts
• High Courts are principal civial courts of original jurisdiction in state along with District Courts which are subordinate to High courts
• High courts excercise their original civil and criminal jurisdiction only if courts subordinate to high court in state are not competent to try such matters for lacl of pecuniary, territorial jurisdiction
• Highest court of appeal in every state that means it can take cases after judgment from Session courts or District courts
• Judges in High Court are appointed by President of India in consultation with Chief Justice of India and governor of statte
• High Court headed by Chief Justice
• Every high court shall consist of Chief Justice and other such judges as President of India may determine from time to time
• It can pass punishment ranging from simple imprisonment to death sentence
