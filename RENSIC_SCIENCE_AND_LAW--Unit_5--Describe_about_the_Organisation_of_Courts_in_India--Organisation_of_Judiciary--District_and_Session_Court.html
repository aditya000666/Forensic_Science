District and Session Court


• Highest court in each district is that of District and Session Judge
• Each state is divided into judicial districts presided over District and Session Judge
• He is highest judicial authority at district level and appointed by State Governor with advice of State Chief Justice
• Known as District Court and Judge is known as District Judge when presides over civil case
• Session Court and Session Judge when presides over criminal case
• Addition to district judge there are number of Additional District Judges and Assistant District Judge depending on workload
• Additional District Judge and court presided have equivalent jurisdiction as District Judge and Court
• District Judge has supervisory control over Additonal Judge and Assistance District Judge including decisions on allocation of work among them
• District Court or Additional Court exercise jurisdiction both on original side and appelate side in civil and criminal matters arising in District
• They can pass punishments from simple imprisonment to death, but must approve by High Court
